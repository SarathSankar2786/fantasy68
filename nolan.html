Christopher Nolan Filmography

A chronological showcase of Nolan's cinematic journey.

Following (1998)

Nolan's debut feature, a noir thriller about a young writer who follows strangers for material, leading him into a dark underworld.

Memento (2000)

A man with short-term memory loss uses tattoos and notes to hunt for his wife's killer in this non-linear narrative.

Insomnia (2002)

A seasoned detective investigates a murder in an Alaskan town where the sun doesn't set, confronting his own moral dilemmas.

Batman Begins (2005)

The origin story of Bruce Wayne's transformation into Batman, facing the League of Shadows and Gotham's corruption.

The Prestige (2006)

Two rival magicians engage in a bitter competition, leading to obsession and tragic consequences.

The Dark Knight (2008)

Batman faces the Joker, a criminal mastermind who plunges Gotham into chaos, testing Batman's moral limits.

Inception (2010)

A skilled thief is given a chance at redemption if he can successfully perform inception: planting an idea into a target's subconscious.

The Dark Knight Rises (2012)

Eight years after the Joker's reign, Batman returns to face Bane, a new terrorist leader threatening Gotham's destruction.

Interstellar (2014)

In a dystopian future, a team of explorers travels through a wormhole in space to ensure humanity's survival.

Dunkirk (2017)

A World War II epic depicting the evacuation of Allied soldiers from the beaches of Dunkirk, told from land, sea, and air perspectives.

Tenet (2020)

A secret agent manipulates the flow of time to prevent World War III in this complex, time-bending thriller.

Oppenheimer (2023)

An intense historical biopic about J. Robert Oppenheimer, the theoretical physicist who spearheaded the Manhattan Project. The film delves into the moral consequences of scientific discovery, political fallout, and the personal torment of a man burdened with the power to destroy the world. With visually stunning sequences and psychological depth, it portrays the making of the atomic bomb and the aftermath that followed.
